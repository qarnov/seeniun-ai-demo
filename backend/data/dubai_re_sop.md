## SECTION 2: TYPES OF PROPERTY OWNERSHIP

### 2.1 Freehold Ownership
- You own the property AND the land permanently
- Can sell, lease, rent, or pass to heirs without restriction
- Available in designated freehold zones only (foreigners can only buy here)
- Most common for overseas investors
- Examples: Dubai Marina, Downtown Dubai, JVC, Business Bay, Palm Jumeirah

### 2.2 Leasehold Ownership
- You own rights to the property for a fixed period (typically 99 years)
- You own the property but NOT the land
- Less common for foreign investors
- Available in certain non-freehold areas

### 2.3 Off-Plan Properties
- Purchased directly from a developer before or during construction
- Lower entry price than ready properties
- Flexible payment plans (e.g., 40/60, 60/40, 80/20)
- High capital appreciation potential by handover
- Protected by RERA escrow system
- Over 60% of Dubai transactions are now off-plan

### 2.4 Ready Properties
- Completed, existing properties
- Immediate occupancy or rental income
- No construction risk
- Title Deed issued immediately
- Higher entry price than off-plan equivalents

## SECTION 3: STEP-BY-STEP BUYING PROCESS

### 3.1 Buying Off-Plan (New Development from Developer)

**Step 1 — Choose Your Property**
Select a project from a RERA-registered developer. Verify the developer's RERA number on the Dubai REST app or DLD website.

**Step 2 — Pay Reservation/Booking Fee**
Typically 5–10% of the property price. This secures your specific unit.

**Step 3 — Sign the Sales Purchase Agreement (SPA)**
Legally binding contract between you and the developer. It includes:
- Purchase price
- Payment schedule and milestones
- Expected completion/handover date
- Unit specifications
- Cancellation terms
Review carefully before signing. You can use a conveyancer for complex purchases.

**Step 4 — Oqood Registration**
Within 90 days of signing the SPA, the developer must register your contract with the Dubai Land Department (DLD) through the Oqood portal. You pay:
- 4% DLD fee on the property value
- AED 580 admin fee
- Small trustee fees (AED 2,000–4,000)
You receive an Oqood certificate — your legal proof of ownership during construction.

**Step 5 — Follow the Payment Plan**
All your installments go into a RERA-monitored escrow account, not directly to the developer. Funds are released to the developer only as construction milestones are verified.

**Step 6 — Track Construction Progress**
Monitor via the Oqood portal or developer app. RERA provides regular project status updates.

**Step 7 — Snagging Inspection**
Before accepting handover, hire a professional snagging inspector to identify defects the developer must fix before you take keys.

**Step 8 — Handover and Title Deed**
Final payment is made. Oqood converts to a full Title Deed at the DLD Trustee Office. You now have permanent legal ownership.

---

### 3.2 Buying a Ready Property (Secondary Market)

**Step 1 — Find Property via Agent**
Work with a RERA-licensed agent. All agents must display their RERA broker card number.

**Step 2 — Sign Form F (MOU)**
Memorandum of Understanding — the contract between buyer and seller. Standard deposit is approximately 10% of purchase price.

**Step 3 — No Objection Certificate (NOC)**
Seller obtains NOC from the developer confirming no outstanding service charges or dues.

**Step 4 — Title Deed Transfer**
At a DLD Trustee Office, the buyer pays:
- 4% DLD transfer fee (typically paid by buyer, sometimes split with seller)
- AED 4,000–4,200 trustee office admin fee
- Agent commission: 2% of property price (standard in Dubai)
Title Deed is issued in your name immediately.

## SECTION 4: ALL FEES EXPLAINED

### 4.1 Dubai Land Department (DLD) Transfer Fee
- **Amount**: 4% of the property purchase price
- **Who pays**: Typically the buyer (sometimes negotiated to be split 50/50)
- **When**: At Oqood registration (off-plan) or Title Deed transfer (ready)
- **Example**: AED 1,000,000 property = AED 40,000 DLD fee

### 4.2 Agency/Broker Commission
**Secondary market (resale properties):**
- Buyer pays 2% to their agent — market standard
- Seller may pay 2% only if they have a signed exclusive listing agreement with an agent
- VAT of 5% applies on top of commission (e.g. 2% of AED 2M = AED 40,000 + AED 2,000 VAT = AED 42,000)

**Off-plan (buying directly from developer):**
- Developer pays the agent commission entirely
- Buyer pays ZERO agent commission on off-plan purchases
- This is one of the biggest financial advantages of buying off-plan

### 4.3 DLD Trustee Office Fee
- Ready properties: AED 4,000–4,200
- Off-plan (Oqood): AED 2,000–4,000

### 4.4 Oqood Admin Fee
- AED 580 fixed fee on top of the 4% DLD fee for off-plan properties

### 4.5 Service Charges (Annual)
- Paid by property owner (or tenant by agreement)
- Varies by building: AED 10–30 per sq.ft per year
- Luxury Downtown/DIFC towers: up to AED 25–30/sq.ft (can eat up 30% of rental income)
- "Chiller Free" buildings: no district cooling charges — better for ROI
- Check the RERA Service Charge Index before buying

### 4.6 Mortgage (If Applicable)
- Banks offer mortgages to foreign investors
- LTV for UAE residents (expats with valid visa): up to 80% on properties under AED 5M (20% down payment)
- LTV for non-residents (overseas buyers): 50–65% (meaning 35–50% down payment required)
- Off-plan mortgages for non-residents: most banks require 50% down payment minimum; some don't offer it at all — cash purchase is standard for non-residents buying off-plan
- Interest rates for non-residents: typically 4.8%–6.5% depending on lender and profile
- Interest rates for residents: typically 4.25%–5.5%
- Consult UAE mortgage brokers (Emirates NBD, HSBC, Mashreq, ADIB, Standard Chartered)

## SECTION 5: OFF-PLAN PAYMENT PLANS

### 5.1 Common Payment Plan Structures
Off-plan properties in Dubai come with flexible payment plans:

**40/60 Plan**
- 40% paid during construction (in installments)
- 60% paid on handover
- Most common structure

**60/40 Plan**
- 60% during construction
- 40% on handover
- Better for developers, slightly less common

**80/20 Plan**
- 80% during construction
- 20% on handover
- Usually higher appreciation before handover

**Post-Handover Payment Plans**
- Some developers offer 2–5 years of payments after you receive keys
- Example: 30% during construction, 70% over 3 years after handover
- Great for investors planning to rent out immediately

### 5.2 What Happens If You Miss a Payment?
- Refer to your SPA for exact terms
- Typically a grace period of 30 days
- After extended default, developer may cancel and refund with deductions
- RERA regulates refund percentages based on construction completion stage

## SECTION 6: KEY LEGAL TERMS & DOCUMENTS

### 6.1 RERA (Real Estate Regulatory Agency)
The government body that regulates Dubai's property market. Responsibilities:
- Licensing all developers, agents, and brokers
- Overseeing off-plan sales and escrow accounts
- Handling disputes between buyers and developers
- Publishing the Service Charge Index
Every legitimate agent has a RERA broker card. Always verify.

### 6.2 DLD (Dubai Land Department)
The master government body overseeing all property registration and ownership records in Dubai. Established 1960, digitized through initiatives like Oqood and the Dubai REST app.

### 6.3 Oqood
- Arabic for "contracts"
- DLD's digital registration system for off-plan property transactions
- Your Oqood certificate = your legal proof of ownership during construction
- Protects against double-selling
- All your payments enter RERA-monitored escrow
- Converts to full Title Deed at handover

### 6.4 Title Deed
- Permanent ownership document
- Issued by DLD after full payment and project completion (off-plan) or at transfer (ready)
- Required for Golden Visa application

### 6.5 SPA (Sales Purchase Agreement)
- Legally binding contract between buyer and developer/seller
- Contains all terms: price, payment schedule, specs, handover date, cancellation
- Must be registered with DLD within 90 days for off-plan

### 6.6 Form F (MOU)
- Memorandum of Understanding for ready property transactions
- Between buyer and seller
- Signed before the formal title deed transfer
- Typically accompanied by 10% deposit

### 6.7 NOC (No Objection Certificate)
- Issued by developer to the seller
- Confirms no outstanding service charges, dues, or obligations on the property
- Required before any property transfer can occur

### 6.8 Escrow Account
- Mandatory for all off-plan developments in Dubai (Law No. 8 of 2007)
- Your payments do NOT go directly to the developer
- Funds held in a regulated account, released only as construction milestones verified
- Protects your money if developer faces financial difficulties

## SECTION 7: GOLDEN VISA THROUGH PROPERTY

### 7.1 What Is the Golden Visa?
A 10-year UAE residency visa available to property investors. Renewable indefinitely as long as you maintain property ownership.

### 7.2 Eligibility Requirements (2026 Updated Rules)
- Minimum property value: **AED 2,000,000** (based on DLD-certified valuation)
- Property must be in a freehold zone registered with DLD
- Developer must be RERA-approved
- **New 2026 rule**: Off-plan properties now qualify — you don't need the full amount paid upfront
- **New 2026 rule**: Mortgaged properties qualify if DLD valuation reaches AED 2M and bank issues NOC
- Multiple properties can be combined to reach AED 2M threshold

### 7.3 What's Changed in 2026
- Previous rule required 50% paid upfront (AED 1M minimum down payment) — **this is now removed**
- Now based purely on DLD valuation reaching AED 2M
- Off-plan from RERA-approved developers now accepted
- Mortgaged properties accepted with bank NOC
- Investor visa (2-year) no longer has AED 750,000 minimum for sole ownership

### 7.4 Step-by-Step Golden Visa Process
1. Purchase qualifying property (AED 2M+ DLD valuation)
2. Ensure Title Deed / Oqood registered in your name
3. Gather documents: passport, Title Deed/Oqood, Emirates ID, visa copy, photos
4. Submit application at Al Manara Center (Cube) or authorized service center
5. Complete medical examination
6. Pay fees (~AED 9,885 total)
7. Receive 10-year residence permit via email

### 7.5 Golden Visa Fees (Approx.)
| Item | Cost |
|------|------|
| DLD fees | AED 4,020 |
| Residency permit confirmation | AED 2,856.75 |
| Emirates ID | AED 1,153 |
| Administrative fees | AED 1,155 |
| Medical examination | AED 700 |
| **Total** | **~AED 9,885** |

### 7.6 Key Benefits
- 10-year renewable residency — no employer sponsor needed
- Sponsor family members (spouse, children)
- No mandatory minimum stay in UAE required
- Access to UAE banking, business ownership, schooling
- Tax-free capital gains — all appreciation is yours

### 7.7 Important Warnings
- AED 2M threshold has zero flexibility — a AED 1.95M property will be rejected
- Off-plan only qualifies if developer is RERA-registered
- Always verify property valuation independently through a RERA-licensed appraiser before purchase

## SECTION 8: RENTAL YIELDS & ROI BY AREA

### 8.1 How to Calculate ROI
**Gross Yield** = Annual Rent / Property Price × 100
**Net ROI** = (Annual Rent – Service Charges – Maintenance – 5% Vacancy) / Total Purchase Cost × 100

Always use Net ROI for real decisions. Two "8% gross yield" deals can deliver very different outcomes after service charges.

## SECTION 10: COMMON INVESTOR QUESTIONS

**Q: Can Indians / foreigners fully own property in Dubai?**
A: Yes. In designated freehold zones (80+ areas), foreigners have 100% ownership with full rights to sell, rent, or inherit.

**Q: Do I need to live in Dubai to own property?**
A: No. You can own property as a non-resident. You also do not need to stay in UAE to maintain your Golden Visa.

**Q: Can I get a mortgage as a foreigner?**
A: Yes, but terms differ by residency status. UAE residents (expats with valid visa) can borrow up to 80% LTV on properties under AED 5M (20% down payment). Non-residents (overseas buyers) are limited to 50–65% LTV, meaning a 35–50% down payment. For off-plan properties, most banks don't offer mortgages to non-residents at all — cash purchase is the norm. Interest rates typically range 4.8%–6.5% for non-residents.

**Q: What is the minimum investment for Golden Visa?**
A: AED 2,000,000 in property (DLD-certified valuation). As of 2026, this can be off-plan or mortgaged property.

**Q: What are the taxes on Dubai property?**
A: Zero. No capital gains tax. No income tax on rental earnings. The only government fees are one-time transaction fees (DLD 4%) and annual service charges.

**Q: What is the difference between Oqood and Title Deed?**
A: Oqood is your temporary ownership certificate during off-plan construction. Title Deed is your permanent ownership document issued after handover and full payment.

**Q: What happens if a developer delays or cancels a project?**
A: RERA regulates this. Your payments are protected in escrow. RERA provides mediation and can compel refunds. Refund amounts depend on construction completion stage.

**Q: Can I sell my off-plan property before completion?**
A: Yes, through "assignment of contract." Typically requires paying 30–40% of property value first (developer-specific threshold), obtaining NOC from developer, and transferring at DLD Trustee Office.

**Q: What documents do I need to buy property in Dubai?**
A: Valid passport copy, signed reservation form, and signed SPA. If buying through a company: trade license, board resolution, and authorized signatory documents.

**Q: How long does the buying process take?**
A: Off-plan: reservation to Oqood in 1–4 weeks. Ready property title deed transfer: typically 7–15 days after MOU signing.

**Q: What is the best area to invest for rental yield?**
A: JVC and Discovery Gardens offer the highest gross yields (7–9%). For capital appreciation combined with yield, Business Bay and Dubai Marina are strong. For luxury capital preservation, Downtown Dubai and Palm Jumeirah.

**Q: Is Dubai's property market safe for overseas investors?**
A: Dubai has one of the most regulated property markets in the world. RERA oversight, mandatory escrow accounts, Oqood registration protecting against double-selling, and DLD transparency make it very secure compared to other emerging markets.
